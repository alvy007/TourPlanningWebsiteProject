Discover the Beauty of Cox's Bazar

The longest unbroken sea beach in the world awaits you

Cox's Bazar, located in southeastern Bangladesh, is a mesmerizing destination renowned for its stunning coastline, stretching over 120 kilometers. Known as the longest natural sandy beach in the world, it attracts millions of visitors annually who come to soak up its beauty and serenity.

Why Visit Cox's Bazar?

Cox's Bazar is not just a beach destination; it's a treasure trove of natural wonders and cultural experiences. The golden sands, turquoise waters, and gentle waves create a perfect backdrop for relaxation and adventure. The area is also home to lush green hills, unique local markets, and friendly communities.

Top Attractions in Cox's Bazar

  • Laboni Beach: The main beach area, perfect for sunrise and sunset views.
  • Inani Beach: A quieter and more serene location, known for its rocky formations.
  • Himchari National Park: A scenic spot featuring waterfalls and panoramic views of the sea.
  • St. Martin's Island: A small coral island nearby, ideal for day trips.
  • Local Markets: Explore traditional crafts, seafood, and souvenirs.

Activities to Enjoy

From beachcombing and swimming to parasailing and beach volleyball, Cox's Bazar offers a variety of activities for all types of travelers. Don't forget to try the delicious local seafood, including fresh prawns and fish curries.

Plan Your Visit

The best time to visit Cox's Bazar is during the winter months (November to February) when the weather is pleasant. With numerous hotels and resorts catering to different budgets, you can find accommodations that suit your needs.
